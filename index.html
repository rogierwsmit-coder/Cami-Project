About CAMI

CAMI is a wearable personal safety platform built to increase the sense of security for women in public spaces. It combines a body-worn device (camera, sensors, alarm) with a mobile app and subscription service. CAMI is focused not only on incident response, but on prevention, peace of mind, and direct support during escalation.

CAMI is privacy-by-design. Footage is visible, temporary (rolling buffer max 48 hours), and strictly intended for personal safety or as incident evidence. AI is used only as a supportive detection layer—no autonomous decision-making or biometric identification—making CAMI legally compliant under GDPR and European legal frameworks.

CAMI is not a surveillance tool or emergency service. It does not guarantee prevention or replace official aid. It is designed as an empowerment tool: the user stays in control while having immediate support within reach.

Strategically, CAMI is a hardware-enabled subscription platform. The hardware acts as an entry point; the core value lies in the software, AI, cloud infrastructure, and recurring subscription. This model is sustainable and internationally scalable.
